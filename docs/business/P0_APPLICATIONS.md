## Tracking the four submissions

Open a spreadsheet (or Notion table) with these columns:

| Brand | Submitted on | Method | Response by | Status | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ARB | YYYY-MM-DD | Online form | +14 days | submitted | — |
| Smittybilt | YYYY-MM-DD | Email | +14 days | submitted | — |
| YETI | YYYY-MM-DD | Sponsor Circle | +21 days | submitted | — |
| BRCC | YYYY-MM-DD | Sponsor Circle | +21 days | submitted | — |

After 14 days with no reply, send one follow-up (never two). Sponsor Circle
proposals especially can take 3–4 weeks for first response — be patient,
the platform has high volume.

## After the first reply

- **They want to chat:** Book a 20-min call. Show them the live build.
  Skip the deck, drive them around your map for 5 minutes. The pitch is
  the product.
- **They want pricing:** Refer to `ONEPAGER.md` — Trailhead $2.5k, Campaign
  $9.5k, White-Label $25k+. For trade-for-promotion offers, propose 60-day
  windows with measurable KPIs (impressions, click-throughs).
- **They say no:** Ask why specifically. The "no, but…" qualifier is gold
  — it tells you what to fix for the next pitch.
- **They ghost:** One follow-up at 14 days. If still nothing at 30 days,
  archive and re-pitch in 6 months with a new portfolio piece.
